Experience

Balloon Club The UMD Payload Program provides a platform for university students to develop scientific payloads to be flown on Weather Balloons in nearspace. My payload team was SATRN whose's goal was to design a payload for the remote and reliable release of payloads on the weather balloons tow. I designed the 3D printed housing for the payload in SolidWorks and integrated it with the Arduino control system. One challenge was making sure that the payload was water resitant enough to keep out water droplets in the atmosphere. (Sadly we never got the chance to test SATRN on a launch due to COVID-19)

Rocket Club The UMD Rocket Club tests and designs rockets that fly in the 10,000 to 30,000 feet range in the atmosphere. This year the group was focused on transitioning from a model rocket engine to a hybrid engine. I worked on the interface for the flight data collection. In the process of this I got some great great experience working with experienced members on the serial port Java libraries for the data collection. My contribution focused on developing the GUI in JavaFX for the for the frontend representation of the data. (Sadly we never got the chance to do a full scale test of the rocket due to COVID-19)
